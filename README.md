The software detects an immediate surge. It cuts the connection to protect your USB port and the breadboard components. You must fix the wiring before the unit allows power to flow again.

**Do I need batteries?**
No. The USB-C connection provides all the energy needed for your experiments.

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

If your BrodBoost-C2 remains inactive, perform these checks:

1. Cable connection: Ensure the USB-C cable clicks into the port.
2. Software status: Confirm the application shows a green light for connected hardware.
3. Breadboard placement: Ensure the pins sit firmly in the power rails of your breadboard.
4. USB port: Try a different USB port on your computer if the current one fails to recognize the device.

If the software reports an error, restart the application. If issues persist, unplug the hardware for ten seconds and reconnect it. This triggers a fresh handshake between the unit and your system. Keep your workspace clear of metal objects to prevent accidental shorts while you adjust your circuit layout.
